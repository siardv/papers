Sensitivity Analysis: Comparing Robust Linear Regression Models with and without Influential Observations

Identifying influential observations through regression diagnostics, such as Cook’s distance, in conjunction with robust estimation methods, such as the MM-estimator, is essential for ensuring the validity and reliability of results in robust linear regression models. A comprehensive analysis of the 13 robust linear regression models using the MM estimator and Cook’s distance, a measure of influence, identified seven data points (observations: 361, 374, 540, 977, 1042, 1175, and 1217) that consistently exceeded the typical threshold of \(\frac{4}{n}\) (where \(n\) is the sample size) in at least four distinct model specifications. Univariate and multivariate outlier analyses conducted before modeling (see p.4) was unable to detect these influential observations due to their unique configuration of predictor and response values, as an observation’s influence in a robust regression model is determined by the combination of its leverage (the extremity of its predictor values) and discrepancy (the degree to which the model poorly fits the observation) rather than its univariate or multivariate distance from the center of the data. To quantify the impact of these influential points, a sensitivity analysis was performed by fitting the robust MM-estimator models with and without the seven observations. The results revealed that the inclusion or exclusion of these data points had a substantial effect on key model parameters and goodness-of-fit measures, such as the coefficient estimates (\(\hat{\beta}\)) and adjusted R-squared (\(\bar{R}^2\)), even when using a robust estimation method designed to mitigate the influence of outliers. This finding provides direct evidence that these observations drive the results considerably, potentially obscuring the proper relationships between the predictors and the response variable. The recurrent high influence of these seven observations across models with different predictors underscores that their impact stems from their overall configuration of predictor and response values rather than a specific relationship with a single predictor. Although the evidence supports the removal of these seven observations, it is crucial to acknowledge the potential limitations and biases introduced by data exclusion, even in the context of robust linear regression models. Therefore, the results are reported with and without these observations to maintain transparency and provide a comprehensive understanding of the data. By prioritizing regression diagnostics in conjunction with robust estimation techniques, analysts can identify truly influential observations and make informed choices to ensure the accuracy and validity of their results. This approach enables a more nuanced understanding of the data. It helps prevent erroneous conclusions based on the undue influence of a small subset of observations, ultimately strengthening the reliability and interpretability of robust linear regression models.
